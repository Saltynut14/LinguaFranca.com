The English alphabet consists of 26 letters. Each letter has an uppercase ("capital letter") and a lowercase ("small letter") form.

B - Uppercase: B, Lowercase: b, Pronunciation: /biː/, Example: Ball

C - Uppercase: C, Lowercase: c, Pronunciation: /siː/, Example: Cat

D - Uppercase: D, Lowercase: d, Pronunciation: /diː/, Example: Dog

E - Uppercase: E, Lowercase: e, Pronunciation: /iː/, Example: Elephant

F - Uppercase: F, Lowercase: f, Pronunciation: /ɛf/, Example: Fish

G - Uppercase: G, Lowercase: g, Pronunciation: /dʒiː/, Example: Goat

H - Uppercase: H, Lowercase: h, Pronunciation: /eɪtʃ/, Example: Hat

I - Uppercase: I, Lowercase: i, Pronunciation: /aɪ/, Example: Ice

J - Uppercase: J, Lowercase: j, Pronunciation: /dʒeɪ/, Example: Jelly

K - Uppercase: K, Lowercase: k, Pronunciation: /keɪ/, Example: Kite

L - Uppercase: L, Lowercase: l, Pronunciation: /ɛl/, Example: Lion

M - Uppercase: M, Lowercase: m, Pronunciation: /ɛm/, Example: Monkey

N - Uppercase: N, Lowercase: n, Pronunciation: /ɛn/, Example: Nest

O - Uppercase: O, Lowercase: o, Pronunciation: /oʊ/, Example: Orange

P - Uppercase: P, Lowercase: p, Pronunciation: /piː/, Example: Penguin

Q - Uppercase: Q, Lowercase: q, Pronunciation: /kjuː/, Example: Queen

R - Uppercase: R, Lowercase: r, Pronunciation: /ɑːr/, Example: Rabbit

S - Uppercase: S, Lowercase: s, Pronunciation: /ɛs/, Example: Snake

V - Uppercase: V, Lowercase: v, Pronunciation: /viː/, Example: Violin

W - Uppercase: W, Lowercase: w, Pronunciation: /ˈdʌbəl.juː/, Example: Whale

Each letter plays a significant role in forming words, sentences, and expressions in the English language. Mastering the pronunciation of each letter is essential for effective communication and literacy.

Lesson 1: Alphabet - Letters O, D, S, and P

Lesson 1: The Alphabet – Letters O, D, S, and P

Welcome to your first ever English lesson! Today, we’ll start with a few important letters of the alphabet: A, B, C, D and T. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

1. Letter A (a)

Pronunciation: /eɪ/

Sound: A makes the sound "ah" or "ay."

    • Onion – Apple – A fruit that is sweet and crunchy.
    • Ant – A small insect.

1. Letter B (b)

Pronunciation: /bi:/

Sound: A makes the sound "buh."

    • Ball – A round object used in sports.
    • Bird – An animal with wings that can fly.

3. Letter C (c)

Pronunciation: /siː/

Sound: C can make a "kuh" sound (like in cat) or an "s" sound (like in city).

    • Cat – A small, furry pet.
    • Car – A vehicle that you drive.

4. Letter R (r)

Pronunciation: /ɑːr/

Sound: R makes the "rrr" sound.

    • Rabbit – A small animal with long ears.
    • Rain – Water that falls from the sky.

5. Letter T (t)

Pronunciation: /tiː/

Sound: T makes the "tuh" sound.

    • Tiger – A large, wild cat with stripes.
    • Table – A piece of furniture with a flat top.
Lesson 2: Alphabet - Letters O, D, S, and P

Lesson 2: The Alphabet – Letters O, D, S, and P

Welcome to your second English lesson! Today, we’ll start with another important letters of the alphabet: O, D, S, and P. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

6. Letter O (o)

Pronunciation: /oh/

Sound: O makes the sound /ɒ/ (as in "hot") or /oʊ/ (as in "go").

    • Onion – a round vegetable with many layers inside each other and a brown, red or white skin.
    • Owl – a bird with a flat face, large eyes, and a small sharp beak.

7. Letter D (d)

Pronunciation: /diː/

Sound: D makes the sound "duh."

  • Dog – A domesticated animal known for being a loyal pet.
  • Doll – A toy figure representing a human being, often used for play.

8. Letter S (s)

Pronunciation: /ɛs/

Sound: S makes the sound "sss."

  • Snake – A long, legless reptile that can be dangerous.
  • Sun – The star at the center of our solar system that provides light and warmth.

9. Letter P (p)

Pronunciation: /piː/

Sound: P makes the sound "puh."

  • Penguin – A flightless bird that lives in cold climates.
  • Pineapple – A tropical fruit with a spiky exterior and sweet, juicy flesh.

Lesson 3: The Alphabet – Letters M, G, and L

Welcome to your third English lesson! Today, we’ll learn three more important letters of the alphabet: M, G, and L. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

10. Letter M (m)

Pronunciation: /ɛm/

Sound: M makes the sound "muh."

  • Monkey – A playful animal that is known for climbing trees.
  • Mango – A sweet tropical fruit with yellow-orange flesh.

11. Letter G (g)

Pronunciation: /dʒiː/

Sound: G makes the sound "guh."

  • Giraffe – A tall animal known for its long neck and legs.
  • Grapes – Small, round fruits that grow in bunches and can be green, red, or purple.

12. Letter L (l)

Pronunciation: /ɛl/

Sound: L makes the sound "luh."

  • Lion – A large wild cat known as the "king of the jungle."
  • Lemon – A sour yellow fruit used in drinks and desserts.
Lesson 4: Alphabet - Letters I, J, K, and N

Lesson 4: The Alphabet – Letters I, J, K, and N

Welcome to your fourth English lesson! Today, we’ll learn four more important letters of the alphabet: I, J, K, and N. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

13. Letter I (i)

Pronunciation: /aɪ/

Sound: I makes the sound "ih."

  • Ice – Frozen water that is cold and slippery.
  • Igloo – A dome-shaped structure made of ice, often used by Inuit people.

14. Letter J (j)

Pronunciation: /dʒeɪ/

Sound: J makes the sound "juh."

  • Jelly – A sweet spread made from fruit and sugar, often used on bread.
  • Juice – A drink made from the extraction of the liquid from fruits or vegetables.

15. Letter K (k)

Pronunciation: /keɪ/

Sound: K makes the sound "kuh."

  • Kite – A toy that flies in the air, typically made of lightweight materials and flown on a string.
  • Koala – A tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia, known for its fluffy ears and nose.

16. Letter N (n)

Pronunciation: /ɛn/

Sound: N makes the sound "nuh."

  • Nest – A structure built by birds to hold their eggs and raise their young.
  • Nose – The part of the face used for smelling and breathing.

Lesson 5: The Alphabet – Letters E, F, H, and U

Welcome to your fifth English lesson! Today, we’ll learn four more important letters of the alphabet: E, F, H, and U. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

17. Letter E (e)

Pronunciation: /iː/

Sound: E makes the sound "eh."

  • Elephant – A large mammal with a trunk and tusks, known for its intelligence.
  • Egg – A round object laid by birds, often eaten as food.

18. Letter F (f)

Pronunciation: /ɛf/

Sound: F makes the sound "fuh."

  • Fish – An aquatic animal that lives in water.
  • Flower – The colorful part of a plant that produces seeds.

19. Letter H (h)

Pronunciation: /eɪtʃ/

Sound: H makes the sound "huh."

  • Hat – A head covering worn for warmth or fashion.
  • House – A building where people live.

20. Letter U (u)

Pronunciation: /juː/

Sound: U makes the sound "uh."

  • Umbrella – A device used for protection against rain or sun.
  • Unicorn – A mythical horse-like creature with a single horn on its forehead.

Lesson 6: The Alphabet – Letters Q, V, and W

Welcome to your sixth English lesson! Today, we’ll learn three more important letters of the alphabet: Q, V, and W. Let’s learn how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

21. Letter Q (q)

Sound: Q makes the sound "kwuh."

22. Letter V (v)

23. Letter W (w)

  • Whale – A large marine mammal.

Lesson 7: Alphabet - Letters X, Y, and Z

Lesson 7: The Alphabet – Letters X, Y, and Z

Welcome to your seventh English lesson! Today, we’ll learn about the final letters of the alphabet: X, Y, and Z. Let’s discover how to pronounce them and see examples of words that begin with each letter.

24. Letter X (x)

Pronunciation: /ɛks/

Sound: X makes the sound "ks."

  • Xylophone – A musical instrument with wooden bars that are struck with mallets.
  • X-ray – A form of radiation used to take images of the inside of the body.

25. Letter Y (y)

Pronunciation: /waɪ/

Sound: Y makes the sound "yuh."

  • Yacht – A medium-sized sailboat equipped for cruising or racing.
  • Yellow – A color resembling that of ripe lemons or sunflowers.

26. Letter Z (z)

Pronunciation: /ziː/ or /zɛd/

Sound: Z makes the sound "zzz."

  • Zebra – An African wild horse with black-and-white stripes.
  • Zoo – A place where animals are kept for public viewing.
